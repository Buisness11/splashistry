Section 01
Finding The Right Clips
The foundation of every great edit
Expand

The clip makes or breaks the edit before you even open your software. The best editors spend as much time finding the perfect clip as they do editing it. Here is exactly where to find the best Curry footage and how to pick the right moment.

Where To Find Curry Clips
+
Best sources for high quality footage
Always go for the highest resolution available
1
YouTube
The best source for full game footage. Search "Curry highlights [year]" or specific game dates. Use 1080p or 4K where available. NBA official channel uploads full game replays.
YouTube: free · Best resolution available
2
NBA app and website
Official NBA clips in high quality. The stats and highlights section has every Curry performance sorted by game and category.
nba.com/stats · Free to browse
3
Twitter and Instagram
Fans and accounts post raw clips constantly right after games. These are often the cleanest isolated moments without commentary over them.
Search: Curry highlights tonight
4
Reddit
r/nba and r/warriors post high quality clips after every game. Often includes isolated angle shots that YouTube does not have.
r/nba · r/warriors · Free
5
KeepVid or SnapSave
Download YouTube clips directly to your camera roll for editing. Always download the highest resolution available.
savefrom.net · Free
Which Curry Moments Hit The Hardest
+
The clips that make edits go viral
Know what to look for before you download
1
Step back threes
The Curry signature. The slow lean back, the elevation, the perfect form. These slow beautifully in editing and look cinematic from almost any angle.
2
Half court shots
The rarest and most electric moment in basketball. If you have a half court clip in your edit people will watch to the end every single time.
3
The shimmy
After a big shot. The crowd reaction combined with the shimmy is one of the most recognizable moments in sports. Editors who use this right get millions of views.
4
Handles and crossovers
Fast paced dribble sequences edit incredibly well to fast music. The footwork is visual and dynamic — perfect for transition heavy edits.
5
Celebration moments
Championships, All-Star games, record-breaking shots. These carry emotional weight that makes viewers feel something — the most shareable edits make people feel something.
How To Pick The Right Clip For Your Edit
+
The one rule of clip selection
Pick the moment that matches the energy of your music. A slow cinematic beat needs a smooth flowing moment — a Curry off-dribble three or a celebration. A hard hitting trap beat needs explosive fast moments — handles, dunks after an and-one, the shimmy. The mismatch between music energy and clip energy is why most edits feel off even when the technical execution is good.
Section 02
Software Setup
CapCut for mobile · Premiere Pro for desktop
Expand

Both CapCut and Premiere Pro can make viral edits. CapCut is faster for mobile posting. Premiere Pro gives you more control and higher quality output. Start with CapCut to learn the fundamentals, move to Premiere Pro when you are ready for the next level.

CapCut Setup — Mobile and Desktop
+
Getting started with CapCut
Free and the fastest way to start
1
Download CapCut
Available on iOS, Android, and desktop. The desktop version has significantly more features than mobile. Use desktop for serious edits.
capcut.com · Free · Desktop version recommended
2
Project settings
Always start a new project at 1080x1920 for TikTok and Reels. Set frame rate to 60fps for smooth slow motion. Never edit at a lower resolution than your final export.
3
Import your clips
Drag clips directly into the timeline. Keep your raw clips organized in a folder before importing — searching through downloads mid-edit wastes time and breaks flow.
4
Essential CapCut features to learn first
Speed curves for slow motion. Keyframes for zoom and pan. Beat sync for music alignment. Chroma key if you have green screen elements. Master these four before anything else.
Premiere Pro Setup — Desktop
+
Getting started with Premiere Pro
Industry standard · More control · Better output
1
Download and setup
Part of Adobe Creative Cloud. $55/month or $20/month for Premiere only. Worth every dollar for serious editors.
Adobe Creative Cloud · adobe.com · Student discount available
2
Sequence settings
New sequence → Digital SLR → 1080p → 59.94fps for smooth slow motion. Name your sequences clearly — you will have multiple open at once.
3
File organization before you start
Create folders: Raw Clips, Music, Sound Effects, Exports. Import everything into these bins before touching the timeline. Disorganized projects slow everything down.
4
Learn the keyboard shortcuts
J K L for playback. C for razor tool. V for selection tool. M for markers. These four shortcuts alone cut your editing time in half.
5
Lumetri Color panel
This is where your color grading lives. Keep it open on a second monitor if you have one. Primary correction first, then creative looks second.
CapCut vs Premiere Pro — which to use when
CapCut: you need to post fast, you are editing on your phone, you want quick trending edits. Premiere Pro: you want the highest quality output, you are doing complex layered edits, you want to build a serious editing career. Most top edit pages use Premiere Pro for their best work and CapCut for quick trending content.
Section 03
Transitions
What separates good edits from viral edits
Expand

Transitions are the signature of an edit page. The right transition at the right moment makes the viewer feel something physically. These are the specific transitions that work best for Curry edits.

The Essential Transitions
+
Master these before anything else
In order of importance
1
The smooth cut
The most underrated transition. A perfectly timed straight cut on the beat is more powerful than any flashy effect. Every transition in this list works better when your cuts are already on beat.
2
Zoom transition
Push in fast on the cut point, push out on the new clip. Creates energy and momentum. Works perfectly on Curry step back moments — zoom into the release, cut to the crowd reaction.
CapCut: Speed · Zoom · Premiere Pro: Scale keyframes
3
Whip pan
Fast horizontal camera movement that matches across two clips. The screen blurs and when it clears you are in the new clip. Looks best when the direction of motion matches.
CapCut: Transition library · Premiere Pro: Warp Stabilizer + blur
4
Match cut
Find two clips with similar shapes, colors, or movements and cut between them. The ball leaving his hand matching to confetti falling. These make people rewatch.
5
Glitch transition
RGB split, screen flash, digital distortion between clips. Use sparingly — one glitch transition per edit maximum. Overusing it makes the edit feel cheap.
CapCut: Effects library · Premiere Pro: Lumetri + offset effect
6
Slow to fast
Start a clip in slow motion, ramp it up to full speed on the beat drop. The Curry step back in slow motion ramping to full speed at the moment of release. This one alone can make an edit go viral.
CapCut: Speed curve · Premiere Pro: Time remapping
Beat Syncing — The Most Important Skill
+

Beat syncing is placing your cuts exactly on the beat of your music. This is the single most important editing skill. An edit with average transitions that is perfectly on beat will outperform an edit with amazing transitions that is off beat every single time.

How to beat sync correctly
The process
1
Mark your beats first
Before placing any clips, listen to your music and mark every beat using markers in your timeline. In Premiere Pro press M on every beat. In CapCut use the beat sync tool.
2
Cut on the 1 and the 3
In most music the emphasis falls on beats 1 and 3. These are your primary cut points. Cuts on 2 and 4 feel slightly off to most listeners without them knowing why.
3
Match energy to beat intensity
Quiet section of the song — slow clips, wide shots, emotional moments. Beat drop — fastest most explosive clips, tightest cuts. The energy of the clip should match the energy of the music at that exact moment.
4
The pre-beat technique
Cut slightly before the beat — one or two frames. The brain processes the visual cut slightly after it sees it so cutting slightly early makes it feel exactly on time. Test this and you will never go back.
Section 04
Color Grading
Turn raw footage into something cinematic
Expand

Color grading is what makes an edit look like it was made by someone who knows what they are doing versus someone who just cut clips together. The right grade makes Curry look like he is in a movie.

The Cinematic Look — Step By Step
+
Primary correction first — always
Fix before you create
1
Fix exposure first
Before any creative grading correct the exposure. Highlights should not be blown out. Shadows should have detail. Get the clip technically correct before making it look good.
Premiere Pro: Lumetri Basic panel · CapCut: Brightness and contrast
2
Color balance
Make sure white is white and skin tones look natural. Curry under stadium lights often has a yellow-orange cast — cool it down slightly with the temperature slider.
3
Add contrast
Increase contrast to separate the darks and lights. This alone makes footage look more cinematic. Do not overdo it — the shadows should still have detail.
Creative grade — the signature look
After primary correction
1
The teal and orange look
The most cinematic color grade. Push shadows toward teal and highlights toward orange. Curry in a Warriors uniform under this grade looks incredible — the blue of the uniform and the gold accent both pop harder.
Premiere Pro: Curves · Lift the green channel in shadows · Push reds in highlights
2
Warriors color grade
Emphasize the blue and gold. Push the overall saturation up slightly. Use selective color to make the Warriors blue pop harder and the gold brighter. The Curry jersey becomes the focal point of every frame.
3
The moody dark grade
Deep shadows, high contrast, slightly desaturated. Best for slow motion step back edits with emotional music. Makes Curry look legendary.
4
Vignette
Add a subtle vignette around the edges of every clip. Draws the eye to the center of frame where Curry is. Keep it subtle — you should not notice it consciously.
LUTs — Instant Cinematic Looks
+
What is a LUT
A LUT is a preset color grade you can apply in one click. Professional editors create LUTs from their grades and apply them consistently across all their edits. This is how edit pages maintain a consistent look. You can buy LUT packs for $10 to $30 or create your own by saving your Lumetri settings as a preset in Premiere Pro.
Best free LUTs for sports edits
Search "free cinematic LUTs" on Google or YouTube. The KineMaster and Dehaze LUT packs are widely used by sports edit pages. Always apply the LUT at 50 to 70% opacity and adjust from there — never full strength.
Section 05
Sound Design
The edit that sounds right hits different
Expand

Most editors focus entirely on visuals and treat sound as an afterthought. The editors who blow up treat sound design as seriously as the visual edit. The right sound makes the viewer feel the edit physically.

Music Selection
+
How to pick the right song
The song choice is 50% of the edit
1
Match the vibe to the moment
Slow emotional piano or strings for championship moments, quiet dominance, and career retrospectives. Hard hitting trap or hip hop for handles, crossovers, and fast paced offense. Upbeat melodic for shimmy moments and celebrations.
2
Use trending audio
TikTok and Instagram heavily favor edits using trending audio. Check the trending sounds page before choosing music. A great edit using a trending sound gets 3x the reach of the same edit with non-trending audio.
3
Find music that has a build and a drop
The best edit songs have a quiet build section and a moment where everything hits at once. Your most explosive clip goes exactly at the drop. This structure makes the edit feel like a story.
4
Where to find music
YouTube Audio Library for copyright-free music. Soundcloud for lesser-known artists who often allow use. CapCut has a built-in licensed music library. For Instagram and TikTok use their in-app music libraries to avoid copyright strikes.
YouTube Audio Library: free · CapCut music: free and licensed
Sound Effects — What The Best Editors Do
+

Sound effects are what separate good edits from great ones. The viewers do not notice them consciously — they just feel that something is different about this edit. That feeling is the sound effects working.

Essential sound effects for sports edits
Layer these under your music
1
Crowd noise
Pull crowd audio from the raw game footage under your music at low volume. The ambient crowd noise makes the edit feel live and real. Keep it at 10 to 20% volume under the music.
2
Swoosh and impact sounds
On every made shot add a subtle swoosh of the ball and the impact of the net. These are nearly inaudible but the brain registers them and makes the edit feel satisfying.
Free sound effects: zapsplat.com · freesound.org
3
Bass boost on the beat
Add a low frequency bass hit on your hardest cuts. This is the physical feeling you get when watching a great edit — the bass hit at the cut point. Keep it subtle.
4
Reverse reverb
Take a sound effect, reverse it, and place it just before the beat drop. Creates tension and anticipation right before the most explosive moment.
Section 06
Going Viral
The distribution strategy
Expand

The best edit in the world gets zero views if you post it wrong. Distribution is a skill as important as editing. Here is exactly how to maximize reach on every platform.

TikTok Strategy For Edit Pages
+
What actually works on TikTok
Post with intention
1
The first 2 seconds are everything
Your hook must hit immediately. Start with your most explosive clip or your most visually interesting moment. Never start with a slow build on TikTok — the viewer is gone in 1.5 seconds if nothing happens.
2
Use trending audio
Check TikTok trending sounds before posting. If your edit can work with a trending sound use it. The algorithm pushes trending audio to more people — it is free reach.
3
Post time matters
7pm to 10pm in your target timezone is peak engagement time. This is when the most people are scrolling. Never post at 2am and expect reach.
4
Caption that creates engagement
Ask a question. "What is the hardest Curry shot of all time?" "Do you remember where you were for this?" Questions get comments. Comments tell the algorithm this content is engaging.
5
Reply to comments with videos
When someone leaves a good comment create a new edit as a reply. TikTok massively boosts video replies to comments. It is free content creation with built-in context.
Instagram Reels Strategy
+
Reels specific tips
Different audience than TikTok
1
Reels are longer tolerated
Instagram viewers watch slightly longer than TikTok. You can have a 15 to 20 second intro build where TikTok would lose them at 3 seconds. Use this.
2
Grid consistency matters
Your Instagram grid is your portfolio. Every edit should feel like it belongs in the same visual world. Consistent color grading across your page signals professionalism.
3
Hashtags
10 to 15 hashtags per post. Mix large general tags with specific niche tags. Always include: #curryedits #nbaedits #stephencurry #warriors #basketballedits
4
Share to Stories immediately after posting
The first 30 minutes after posting are critical for the algorithm. Sharing to your story immediately drives your existing followers to the post which boosts the algorithm push.
The Viral Edit Formula
+
What every viral sports edit has in common
A clip that makes people feel something they did not expect to feel. A song choice that perfectly matches the emotion of the moment. At least one transition or moment that makes the viewer rewatch. A caption or hook that creates a comment. The goal of every edit is to make someone feel something strong enough that they stop scrolling and either comment, share, or rewatch. One of those three things and the algorithm does the rest.
Section 07
Build Your Edit Page
Turn a hobby into a brand
Expand

The difference between an edit account and an edit brand is consistency, identity, and strategy. Here is how to build something people come back to.

Page Branding
+
Make your page look intentional
First impressions matter
1
Profile picture
Your logo or a still from one of your best edits. High contrast, readable at small size, instantly recognizable.
2
Bio
Tell people exactly what you make and for who. Three lines maximum. Example: "Curry edits. The best ones. New drops daily."
3
Consistent watermark
Add a subtle watermark to every edit — your handle in a small corner. When people repost your content without credit the watermark does the marketing for you.
4
Post schedule
Pick a posting frequency and stick to it. Daily is ideal. Every other day is acceptable. Weekly is not enough to build real momentum.
Content Strategy
+
What to post and when
A repeatable system
1
React to real time moments
Post an edit within 2 hours of a big Curry performance. The internet is searching for content about that moment right now. Being first with quality content captures that wave.
2
Evergreen highlight edits
Career best moments, all-time plays, anniversary edits of legendary games. These get shared constantly because they are always relevant regardless of when someone sees them.
3
Collab edits
Edit with other edit pages in your niche. You share their audience, they share yours. Tag each other. This is the fastest organic growth strategy for edit pages.
4
Behind the scenes content
Show your editing process. Speed runs of you building an edit. Tool tutorials. "How I made this edit" breakdowns. This builds a second layer of audience — people who want to learn not just watch.
Section 08
Monetize Your Page
Turn your audience into income
Expand

An edit page with a loyal audience is a business. Here is every real way to make money from the page you are building.

Brand Deals
+

Brand deals are the most common way edit pages make money. Companies pay you to feature their product in your content. The bigger your engagement the more you can charge.

How to get brand deals
You do not need millions of followers
1
What brands pay for edit pages
Sports gear brands, energy drinks, gaming peripherals, streaming services, sports betting platforms, NBA merchandise brands. Any brand that wants to reach a young male sports audience.
2
When to start outreaching
Once you hit 10K followers with consistent engagement you can start pitching. Engagement rate matters more than follower count to brands.
3
What to charge
10K followers: $50 to $150 per post. 50K followers: $200 to $500 per post. 100K followers: $500 to $1,500 per post. These are starting points — negotiate based on your specific engagement.
4
How to pitch
DM the brand directly or find their influencer marketing email. Send your page, your average views, and a one line pitch about why your audience matches their customer.
Selling Your Own Digital Products
+

This is the highest margin income stream available to an edit page. You create it once and sell it forever. Your audience already trusts your editing skills — they will pay to learn them.

What edit pages can sell
Products your audience wants
1
Editing preset packs
Your signature color grade as a downloadable LUT or Premiere Pro preset. Sell for $10 to $30. Takes one afternoon to create and sells indefinitely.
Sell on Gumroad or Whop
2
Transition packs
Your custom transitions packaged as a Premiere Pro or CapCut template. $15 to $50. Some edit pages make thousands per month from transition packs alone.
3
Edit tutorials
A detailed walkthrough of how you make your signature style edit. $20 to $49. Your most engaged followers will pay for direct access to your process.
4
1 on 1 coaching
Private editing sessions over screen share. $50 to $150 per hour. High value, limited quantity, builds the most direct relationships with your biggest fans.
Affiliate Income
+
Passive income from tools you already use
Sign up for affiliate programs for every tool you use — Adobe, CapCut, any editing software. Every time you mention or recommend a tool in your content include your affiliate link. You make a commission on every person who signs up through your link. This is completely passive and adds up significantly at scale.
